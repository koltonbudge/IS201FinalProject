Why I like wake surfing

Wake surfing is fun because you can ride a continuous wave behind the boat and improve tricks over time. It’s also a great way to hang out with friends on the lake catching the rays and listening to great music.

What Is Wakesurfing?

Wakesurfing is a water sport where a rider surfs the wave created by a boat without being directly pulled by a rope. The rope is only used to stand up and get into position. Once you’re in the “sweet spot” of the wave, you toss the rope back into the boat and ride hands-free.

It feels like surfing an endless wave—no paddling, no waiting on the ocean, no chasing sets. The boat continuously generates a clean, surfable wake that you can ride for as long as you want.

How It Works

The Boat Creates a Wave

Modern wake boats use ballast (water weight) and onboard surf systems to shape a steep, powerful wave on one side of the boat.

You Start With a Rope

The rider sits in the water with feet on the board, holding a short wakesurf rope. As the boat accelerates slowly (around 10–12 mph), the rider stands up.

You Drop the Rope

Once you’re balanced in the wave, the rope goes slack—you simply toss it back into the boat.

You Surf Freely

Shift your weight to speed up, slow down, carve, or perform tricks. It feels incredibly smooth and natural.

Why Try Wakesurfing

Beginner-Friendly

  • Slower boat speeds make falls gentle
  • Easy learning curve—many people stand up on day one
  • Boards are stable and forgiving

Low Impact

  • No hard yanking from a rope like wakeboarding
  • You can ride for hours without feeling wrecked the next day

Super Social

  • Everyone hangs out on the boat watching and cheering
  • More “good vibes with friends” than a high-pressure sport

Accessible Almost Anywhere

You don’t need the ocean—just a wakesurf-capable boat and a lake.

Endless Progression

You can keep it mellow or get advanced with tricks like:

  • Shuvs
  • 360s
  • Grabs
  • Airs

Equipment You Need

  • Wakesurf-specific boat (inboard or forward drive — never stern drive or outboard)
  • Wakesurf board (surf, skim, or hybrid style)
  • Wakesurf rope
  • Life jacket (PFD)
  • Spotter in the boat

Is Wakesurfing Safe?

Yes—when done correctly. Since the boat uses an inboard-style prop, the propeller sits far from the rider. The key is to never wakesurf behind boats with exposed props (outboards or stern drives). Always wear a life jacket and keep the rope clear of your body.

What Does Wakesurfing Feel Like?

  • Flying across the water, but calmly
  • Effortless
  • Addicting
  • Like surfing but easier and smoother

The moment you drop the rope and feel the wave push you… you’re hooked.
